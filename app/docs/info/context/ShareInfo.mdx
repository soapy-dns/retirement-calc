Shares, also known as stocks or equities, are seen as among the riskiest assets, as stock markets can be highly unpredictable.

Fairly conservative rates of return might be.

- Growth of 3%
- Income (dividends) of 3%

Dividends will be subject to income tax. This will be calculated for you.

Any growth could result in capital gains tax when the asset is sold. This is not calculated automatically, but could be manually included in the cost of selling.

_Note_ These are long term values. They will go down in value at some point. if this is at the beginning of your retirement period this could have a major impact on your savings.
